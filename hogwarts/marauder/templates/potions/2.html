    Veritaserum was used when Albus Dumbledore found out that Barty Crouch Jr. was disguised as Alastor Moody, the Defence Against the Dark Arts teacher. Dolores Umbridge unsuccessfully tried to have Harry Potter drink Veritaserum, however she had received a fake version of the potion from Severus Snape, and Harry had secretly dumped out the potion instead of drinking it.

    There is an un-named antidote to Veritaserum. Also, Veritaserum's powers can be circumvented with Occlumency. Therefore, this potion cannot be considered a reliable indicator of innocence or guilt in a trial. The potion is also forbidden to be used against students at Hogwarts, much to Snape's disappointment.
